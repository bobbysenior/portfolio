Who I am

I'm Thomas, an engineering student who likes computer science a little too much. I spend my time building small tools, breaking things to understand how they work, and learning whatever the next project happens to require.

Why « al3ph_zero »

The name is a play on ℵ0 (aleph-null), the symbol mathematicians use for the smallest infinity — the size of the set of natural numbers, the first of Cantor's transfinite cardinals.

I like what it stands for: a starting point that is already infinite. Zero is where you begin; aleph-null is how far you can go. It felt like the right metaphor for someone who is still at the beginning but curious about everything — with a bit of leetspeak (« al3ph ») on top, because old habits die hard.

As you can imagine, I'm a math enthusiast, hence I have the obligation to make mathematical references in the content I post online.

How I work

I like building things that are simple before they are clever. I would rather ship something small that I fully understand than something large that I only half control. Most of my projects start as a curiosity and grow only as far as they stay fun to maintain.

I care about clear code, honest documentation, and tools that respect the people who use them.

On artificial intelligence

AI is the most interesting tool of my generation, and also the easiest to misuse. I'm still studying computer science, and I think relying on AI can be detrimental to the learning process if it isn't used carefully. I like coding, and I don't want a machine to do it for me — in that sense, I see myself as a code artisan.

That said, I recognize that AI has revolutionized the industry, and I understand its importance in modern development, so I'm happy to use it in a professional context. For my own projects, I always make it clear when AI was involved. But I still code by hand, because I like it.
